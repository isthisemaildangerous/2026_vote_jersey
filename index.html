About this quiz

How the Voter Match works

Jersey's 2026 election has 92 candidates across Senator, Connétable, and Deputy roles — with Senators being the only candidates every voter can choose. Reading all 92 manifestos and attending every hustings would take days. This quiz is designed to give you a structured, evidence-based shortcut: not to tell you who to vote for, but to help you understand which candidates broadly align with the things you care about most.

📋

How the questions were chosen

The 13 questions were derived from reading the full 2026 Jersey Election manifesto document published at vote.je, which contains statements from all 92 candidates. Each question covers a policy area that appeared consistently across multiple candidates' manifestos — meaning it's a genuine point of debate in this election, not an artificial one.

The topics chosen are: Cost of Living, Housing, Government Spending, Healthcare, Economy, Young People, Environment, Population & Immigration, Government Accountability, Elderly & Social Care, Children & Families, Community Safety, and External Relations.

For each topic, four answer options were written to represent the real spread of positions held by candidates — from more interventionist approaches (e.g. removing GST from food) to more market-oriented ones (e.g. increasing competition). The options are deliberately non-partisan and not labelled by candidate, so your answers reflect your genuine preferences rather than brand loyalty.

ℹ️

The contextual information

Each answer option has an information button (ℹ) that expands a fact panel. These panels were researched and written to give neutral, factual context about each policy option — drawing on publicly available sources including States Assembly debates, the Fiscal Policy Panel's reports, the Policy Centre Jersey's research, government statistics, and the candidates' own manifesto statements.

The intent is to make sure your answers are informed choices, not reactions to how an option is phrased. For example, knowing that removing GST from food would cost around £10m/year and has been debated eight times in the States Assembly gives you the context to judge whether it's a realistic priority — rather than simply ticking "yes, cheaper food sounds good."

⚖️

How candidate scores are calculated

Each of the 17 Senatorial candidates was assigned a stance score of 0, 1, 2, or 3 for every answer option across all 13 questions, based on careful reading of their individual manifesto statements:

3 / StrongThis is a headline manifesto commitment — the candidate explicitly advocates this position 2 / ModerateThe candidate's manifesto broadly supports this direction, even if not their primary focus 0 / NoneThe candidate does not advocate this position, or advocates a conflicting one

Your answers are then matched against these stances, weighted by the importance ratings you gave each topic. So if you rated Housing at 5/5 and Economy at 1/5, a candidate's strong housing position counts for much more in your score than their economic stance.

Crucially, scores are normalised against each candidate's own maximum. A candidate who has strong manifesto positions on 4 topics and moderate ones on the rest has a lower theoretical ceiling than one with strong positions everywhere — so the score always represents "how well did this candidate do relative to how well they could have done with a voter like you?", rather than comparing everyone against an impossible perfect score. This means picking all of a candidate's best-matched answers gives them 100%.

The "Why this match?" explanation for each candidate is generated by Claude AI (Anthropic), which is given your specific answers and alignment data and asked to write a concrete, second-person justification referencing the actual policy areas — not a generic summary.

🎯

What this is — and isn't

This quiz is designed to be a starting point, not a conclusion. Jersey's election system is complex — you vote for candidates across multiple roles (Senator, Connétable, Deputy), and a candidate's effectiveness in office depends on far more than their policy positions: their experience, judgment, relationships, and ability to work within the States Assembly all matter enormously.

The stance scores assigned to each candidate are interpretations of their manifesto text, made in good faith but not verified by the candidates themselves. Manifestos are necessarily brief — a candidate's real position on any issue is likely more nuanced than a single score suggests.

The goal is to make it easier to narrow down which candidates are worth reading more about — not to replace that reading. Use your results as a shortlist, then visit vote.je to read the full manifestos, watch the hustings recordings, and make your own informed decision on 7 June.

🔒

Data and privacy

Your answers are stored anonymously — no name, email, IP address, or any identifying information is collected. The only data saved is your 13 answers (as single letters A–D) and your optional age group, along with a timestamp. This data is used solely to power the "how do others answer?" comparison feature.

The quiz is built as a single self-contained HTML file. It does not use cookies, tracking pixels, or any third-party analytics. The AI justification feature routes through a Cloudflare Worker — your prompt is processed and immediately discarded, never stored.
